# Chief Technology Officer (CTO)

## Responsibilities
Accountable for the reliability, availability, and recoverability of technology services. Owns service assurance, including Service Recovery, Disaster Recovery, Business Continuity, and Cyber Recovery capabilities.

## Primary Interests
- Service availability and stability
- Proven recoverability under stress
- Reduction of operational and cyber risk
- Audit-ready evidence of resilience

## Challenges
- Increasing frequency and complexity of outages
- Fragmented recovery processes across teams
- Limited proof that DR, BC, and cyber recovery will work in real incidents

## Key RBM Features
- Executable service, DR, and cyber recovery runbooks
- Real-time recovery orchestration and visibility
- Capture of actual recovery metrics (RTO/RPO)
- Evidence packs for regulators and auditors

## Before vs After
**Before:** Reactive assurance based on plans and attestations  
**After:** Proactive, evidenced service resilience

## Value Proposition
RBM gives CTOs confidence that services can be restored predictably after disruption, supported by real execution evidence.

## Regulatory & Assurance Drivers
- FCA/PRA Operational Resilience (service continuity)
- DORA ICT resilience and cyber recovery
- ISO 22301 / ISO 27001 principles

## Buying Triggers
- Major service outages or near misses
- Regulatory findings on DR/BC or cyber recovery
- Increased cyber threat landscape

## Likely Objections
- “We already test DR annually.”
- “This overlaps with existing tooling.”

## Messaging & Positioning
RBM is the execution and evidence layer that underpins service assurance and recoverability.
