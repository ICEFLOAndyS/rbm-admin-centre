# Head of Procurement
## Responsibilities
Owns procurement policy, sourcing strategy, and supplier selection governance. Ensures compliant, value-for-money procurement aligned to organisational and regulatory requirements.

## Primary Interests
- Commercial value and cost control
- Contractual risk management
- Supplier compliance and governance
- Auditability of procurement decisions

## Challenges
- Technology purchases bypassing formal procurement
- Poorly articulated requirements from delivery teams
- Difficulty comparing non-standard SaaS offerings

## Key RBM Features
- Clear articulation of RBM scope and value
- Standardised pricing and licensing models
- Evidence of compliance and governance alignment

## Before vs After
**Before:** Bespoke negotiations and unclear value propositions  
**After:** Structured evaluation with transparent commercial and governance outcomes

## Value Proposition
RBM simplifies technology procurement by providing a clear, defensible value case aligned to regulated-industry expectations.

## Procurement Role
**Qualifier / Enabler**

## Buying Triggers
- Large-scale change or resilience programmes
- Regulatory scrutiny of supplier governance
- Need to standardise execution tooling

## Likely Objections
- “This looks like consultancy rather than software.”
- “We already have enterprise tooling.”

## Messaging & Positioning
RBM is a governed, enterprise SaaS capability that reduces delivery risk and operational cost.
