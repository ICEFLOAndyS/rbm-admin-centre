# Chief Information Officer (CIO)

## Responsibilities
Owns enterprise technology strategy in support of business growth, transformation, and innovation. Accountable for successful delivery of major change initiatives while balancing risk, cost, and regulatory obligations.

## Primary Interests
- Enabling business strategy and innovation
- Predictable delivery of large-scale change
- Reduction of change-related business risk
- Executive and board-level confidence

## Challenges
- High failure rates in complex change programmes
- Limited visibility of execution risk until late in delivery
- Disconnect between strategic intent and operational execution
- Regulatory scrutiny of technology-driven change

## Key RBM Features
- Enterprise-wide runbook governance for major change
- Programme and portfolio readiness analytics
- Dependency-aware orchestration across business and IT
- Objective evidence for go/no-go decisions

## Before vs After
**Before:** Innovation slowed by risk-averse change governance  
**After:** Confident, data-driven change execution at scale

## Value Proposition
RBM enables CIOs to deliver innovation and transformation faster and more safely by turning execution risk into measurable, manageable data.

## Regulatory & Assurance Drivers
- FCA/PRA Operational Resilience (change governance)
- DORA ICT change and resilience expectations

## Buying Triggers
- Major digital transformation programmes
- Repeated change-related incidents or delays
- Board concern over technology execution risk

## Likely Objections
- “This feels operational rather than strategic.”
- “We already have programme management tooling.”

## Messaging & Positioning
RBM provides CIOs with executive-level control and confidence over technology change execution.
